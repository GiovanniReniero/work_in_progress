Terre di Cerealto

Cerealto is a small elevation located at the foot of the Piccole Dolomiti ("Small Dolomites") mountain range in northern Italy. It is nestled between the Veneto and Trentino regions, both renowned as bastions of excellent wine. While the Piccole Dolomiti may not be as internationally famous as the Dolomites, a larger mountain range further north in the Italian Alps, they offer beautiful landscapes, hiking trails, and outdoor recreational opportunities for visitors to enjoy. These mountains are increasingly gaining popularity for housing boutique wineries at the foot of their slopes, where ideal conditions produce exciting wines that are a delight to taste and savor.

Cerealto sits at an elevation of 700m, just east of the Valle dell'Agno, with stunning views of the Paubio Massif (2330m) and Mount Marana (1550m). Its soils are predominantly of tertiary volcanic origin, composed of underwater lava flows (basalt) rich in potassium, sodium, magnesium, and iron. Today, these iron-rich soils weather into fertile clays that color the landscape with hues ranging from light orange to deep red and sometimes even blueish tinges. Never boring!

Terre di Cerealto is a new winery run by Massimo and Silvestro, wine-producing visionaries who recognized the immense potential of Cerealto's terroir. They now helm a promising "Cantina" that produces refined still wines and brilliant sparkling whites. The entire production process is firmly based on respecting the environment and minimizing impact on the habitat and existing ecosystems. Terre di Cerealto is ICEA certified!

Only PIWI grape varieties, including Johanniter and Bronner, are grown, along with smaller amounts of Souvignier Gris. Grape cultivation follows traditional Veneto methods, and harvesting is strictly performed by hand, with each bunch carefully examined before being accepted for processing.

Though the production is small, the quality is delightful, and Bubbly Bandits believes that the Terre di Cerealto winery is a winner!

Terre di Cerealto EDELE: Dry White Wine (Bronner, Johanniter, Souvigner Gris)

Lovely fresh, complex and clear notes of lime, blossom, green apple, white pepper and citrus zest. Lingering acidity and refreshing aftertaste of apricots.

Ideal as an aperitif and goes well with sea food or asian cuisine such as sushi. Also a good choice for poultry dishes, dairy products and fresh cheeses.

Terre di Cerealto LAISE' Extra Brut, Traditional Method, 24 Months on the lees. Bronner, Johanniter & Souvigner Gris

Fine notes of herbs, apple and peaches. Elegant, refined it yields an enveloping attack on the palate. A clean saline finish. Excellent as an aperitif on its own but at its best with seafood, shellfish or mature cheese.
