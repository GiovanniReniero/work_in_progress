Gentili

The Gentili winery was founded in 1979 by Carlo Gentili, who with a goal, limited funds and tenacity, gradually built a wine business bringing back a 15th century estate to it's former glory. Located in the hills around Lake Garda, Carlo planted new vineyards and embraced traditional Veronese wine making techniques (Pergola Veronese).

Today the the estate is thriving, Carlo's son Enrico manages his father's venture and many of the vines have reached maturity aging between 20 and 60. Harvesting is still rigorously accomplished by hand in early autumn and grapes are carefully selected for higher end produce. The red grape varieties Corvina, Rondinella, Molinara grow vigorously in proximity of the lake and together create the blend that yields Chiaretto, a high quality rosé wine, well-regarded among wine enthusiasts.

The Gentili winery, although not yet fully "organic certified", is working hard towards this goal. Enrico believes that agriculture should respect the environment and limit the disruption to ecosystems. The Gentili estate promotes local biodiversity, soil conservation, the protection of water resources and wooded areas.

Bardolino Chiaretto from Gentili is a light and pleasant blend of red grapes, typical of the shores of Lake Garda, with scents of delicate flowers, cherries and wild strawberry.

Perfect for pizza or poultry based dishes or even enjoyable on its own with some ice.
