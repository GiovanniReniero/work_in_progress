Poggio delle Grazie

On a gently low sloping hill in the south of Lake Garda lies the vineyard of Poggio delle Grazie. On clear days from the highest point you can admire the reflection of the sun on the surface of the lake from afar and it breath taking.

The road driving to the winery crosses the vineyards of Cortese, Garganega and Corvina grapes passing in front of a tiny church built in 1947 to give thanks to the Virgin Mary for saving the living family from the bombing of Second World War. In fact, in the surrounding areas numerous war relics. were found, thus the name POGGIO delle GRAZIE, The Small Hill of Graces and their logo is the result of a sstylised interpretation of the window placed on the church facade, in addition to a heart to remember the ex-voto placed in the chapel.

Blush color with orangish reflections. Fine and persistent effervescence. Hints of mandarin and peaches. The taste is savory and enveloping, sweet sensations accompanied with a hint of minerals.

Great with Pizza or white meat.

Rosè color with violet reflections, with scent sof small red fruits and eucalyptus. TThere is a fine balance between softness and mineral flavor with hints of black currant and citrus.

Ideal for pizza and fish based dishes or with a curry.
