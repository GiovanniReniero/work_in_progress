Giannitessari

Giannitessari is a boutique winery run by Gianni Tessary and his family. Located in the village of Roncà, in the Alpone Valley, the Tessari estate holds a prime position at the heart of the Veneto region between the towns of Verona and Vicenza. The Tessari vineyards cover different terrains surrounding Roncà, each with distinct soil compositions set at different elevations.

The variation in terroir allows the Giannitessari winery to produce a range of different wines. They craft traditional white Soave from the Garganega grape, known for its excellence, and produce exceptional sparkling Durello fizz from the exclusive Durella variety.

The Durella vine is a marvelouse indigenous grape, cultivated exclusively to this specific area of Veneto. The volcanic, tertiary soil of this part of the world is particularly rich in minerals, that yield unique acidity and exquisite fine bubbles to Durello sparkling wine.

The Durasena, an ancestor of the Durella grape, is mentioned in literature as far back as 1292. Evidence of this variety's existence in the Durella growing area dates back to prehistoric times, as is proven by local fossil discoveries of ancient "Vitis" wine varieties, ancestors to the present-day "Vitis Vinifera".

The Lessini Mountains, are rich in meadows and woodland that host a rich fauna of insects and microorganisms. The fauna lives in symbiosis with the local vineyards in an ecosystem of natural biodiversity. The volcanic soil, is rich in minerals, the climate is fresh and the temperature range ideal for grape growing.

All the above expresses itself marvelously in Giannitessari’s wines.

Giannitessari was also the first winery in the Veneto region to enrol in the PIWI International Association. This organisation collects and exchanges information between grape growers of PIWI varieties and research centers, with the goal of promote awareness and knowledge on PIWI breeding techniques.

PIWI, in German Pilzwiderstandfähig, in English translates to "fungus resistant grape". The Giannitessari winery has a PIWI wine among it's produce, specifically from the fungus resistent Solaris grape variety.

Giannitessari Lessini Durello Brut is matured via the Traditional Method and spends 36 months on the lees. Grape variety is 100% Durella. Vegan and Biodiversity friendly.

Long lasting bubbles with a certain creaminess, combined with a rich minerality. The color is bright yellow, the perlage is thick and continuous. The aroma is complex of citrus, pineapple and white pulp fruit together with white wildflowers, with a sweet trace of almonds and toasted bread.

Perfect with fish and shell fish, in particular with oysters, but to be honest, its fabulous with fried food as well.

Giannitessari Alpone Durello Giallo has a straw yellow color, is delicate with fresh fragrance of wildflowers and white pulp fruit. The flavor is velvety, lively, slightly fruity with a toasted hint and long lasting ending.

Pairs well with all sorts of appetizers, great as a welcoming drink, also good with sweets, pudding and cake.

Giannitessari Alpone Durello Blu has a dry slightly fruity taste. The fragrances evoke wildflowers and hints of apple and pear. It is a lively wine, with a bright straw yellow color and fine grain long lasting perlage.
