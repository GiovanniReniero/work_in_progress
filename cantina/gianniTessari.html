Giannitessari

Giannitessari is the winery run by Giannitessari and his family located in Roncà, in the heart of Valle d’Alpone, in Verona, though also very near Vicenza. They are right in the heart of the Veneto region and their vineyard cover there different territories.

Thanks to this variety, Giannitessari produces great wines in various areas like Soave and the mountains of Lessini for their Durello sparkly.

Durello is a marvellous expression and result of the indigenous vine variety and grapes Durella, only grown in this specific land of volcanic soil, once covered by the sea and where minerality and ultimately fine bubbles dominate.

The Durasena, ancestor of the Durella grape, has been quoted in literature since 1292. The first evidence of the variety dates back to the prehistorical ages, as evidenced by the findings of the fossil amperides, ancestors of the nowadays Vitis vinifera, occurred in Vestenanova di Bolca one of the largest historical places in Europe famous for its fossils

The Lessini Mountains, are rich in meadows and woodland that host a variety of insects and microorganisms. These live in symbiosis with the local vineyards allowing for an ecosystem of natural biodiversity. The volcanic soil, is rich in minerals, the climate is fresh and the temperature range ideal for grape growing.

All the above expresses itself magnificently in Giannitessari’s wines.

Giannitessari is also the first winery in the Veneto region enrolled in the PIWI International Association that promotes the collection and exchange of information between research institutes, grape growers of PIWI varieties, with the goal of spread the knowledge about them.

PIWI, in German Pilzwiderstandfähig, in English means fungus resistant grape varieties and Giannitessari has one wine from Piwi variety Solaris, namely Rebellis orange wine.

Giannitessari Lessini Durello Brut is matured via the Traditional Method and spends 36 months on the lees. Grape variety is 100% Durella. Vegan and Biodiversity friendly.

Long lasting bubbles with a certain creaminess, combined with a rich minerality. The color is bright yellow, the perlage is thick and continuous. The aroma is complex of citrus, pineapple and white pulp fruit together with white wildflowers, with a sweet trace of almonds and toasted bread.

Perfect with fish and shell fish, in particular with oysters, but to be honest, its fab with deep fried food too

Giannitessari Alpone Durello Giallo has a straw yellow color, is delicate with fresh fragrance of wildflowers and white pulp fruit. The flavor is velvety, lively, slightly fruity with a toasted hint and long lasting ending.

Pairs well with all sorts of appetizers, great as a welcoming drink, also fab with pudding and cake.
