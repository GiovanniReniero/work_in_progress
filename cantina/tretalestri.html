Tre Talestri

Talestri, the Queen of the Amazons, possessed a unique blend of tenacity and elegance. Similarly, the three sisters Anna, Lisa, and Francesca, exude these remarkable qualities. Raised within a wine making family their father, Francesco Marcato, and his brothers produced exquisite Soave and Durello wines. This family legacy was nurtured in Lavagno, a charming town in Verona, located in the Eastern region of Valpolicella.

The Talestri project, however, emerged as a fresh beginning for the sisters on their estate situated 160 meters above sea level. What sets their vineyards apart is the unique composition of the soil. It is primarily composed of sedimentary limestone, which is characteristic of the Valpolicella region, interspersed with occasional basalt outcrops, a common occurrence in the eastern part of the Verona province. Facing south, their vineyards bask in the warm embrace of the sun throughout the day. Yet, they are also blessed by a gentle constant breeze, offering respite and coolness. As night falls, the vineyards are refreshed by the invigorating mountain winds, ensuring the optimal conditions for the grapes to thrive and develop their flavors.

Since 2020, the wines produced by the sisters have achieved the prestigious SQNPI certification, which stands for the National Quality System for Integrated Production. This certification is a testament to their unwavering dedication to sustainability. Their commitment is evident through their continuous efforts to minimize the use of chemical substances and reduce energy consumption. In their vineyards, the slopes have been adorned with a variety of plants that emit captivating essences and fragrances, attracting bees and promoting biodiversity. Additionally, they have implemented the practice of sowing green manure crops. This enriches the soil with organic matter and encourages the growth of fibrous roots, further enhancing the health of the vineyard ecosystem. To minimize their environmental impact, Anna, Lisa, and Francesca rely on energy from renewable sources. By harnessing renewable energy, they contribute to a greener future while ensuring their operations align with sustainable practices. Furthermore, they employ solely organic pest control methods, prioritizing natural solutions that preserve the ecosystem's balance and protect the health of their vineyards. Through their SQNPI certification and their conscious choices, the three sisters demonstrate their commitment to producing wines that harmonize with nature, embodying the true essence of sustainable winemaking.
