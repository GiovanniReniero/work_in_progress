Tre Talestri

Talestri was the Queen of the Amazons and these three sisters, Anna, Lisa and Francesca have definitely got the tenacity and elegance of three sovereigns. They were born and bred in the winery where their father Francesco Marcato produced Soave and Durello wine with his brothers, in Lavagno in Verona in the Veneto region, East Valpolicella.

The Talestri project, though, was created for a fresh start in their estate located at 160 metres above sea level, stretching out to embrace the San Briccio hill, an ancient underwater volcano. The special quality of their vineyards is the make-up of the land consisting of sedimentary limestones, typical of the Valpolicella area, with occasional basalt outcrops, a common feature of the eastern part of the province of Verona. The vineyards face south and make the most of the sun-drenched warmth of the day, cooled by a constant breeze and refreshed at night by mountain winds.

From 2020, their wines obtained the SQNPI certification (- National Quality System for Integrated Production) certifying the company's pledge to sustainability. With relentless commitment, they have focused on reducing chemical substances and energy consumption. The slopes have been covered in essences and fragrances that attract bees, and they have sown green manure crops to enrich the soil with organic substances and fibrous roots. They also use energy from renewable sources and only organic pest control methods
