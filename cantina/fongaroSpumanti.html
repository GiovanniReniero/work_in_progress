Fongaro Spumanti

In the easternmost valley of the province of Verona, Italy, there is an estate determined to invest in Traditional Method sparkly and organic farming. This is the story of Fongaro Winery, founded in 1975 by Guerrino Fongaro in the town of Roncà, within the Lessinia Regional Natural Park, a volcanic area also famous for the variety of fossils located throughout the Lessini Mountains, especially those of Bolca.

The first gamble was to grow the indigenous Lessinia grape variety, Durella. This vine of ancient origin was “rediscovered” in the late 1960s and was considered mountainous, rustic and wild. The juice from its grapes was vinified through maceration with skins producing “hard wine for hard people.” It was often watered down or used for blending to increase the acidity of other wines.

Durella grapes is particulary indicated for sparkly wines and gives birth to the amasing Durello sparkly wine that Fongaro produces exclusively through Traditional method. This is a vinification method that, more than any other, elevates and highlights the distinct the quality of Durella grapes.

Around 1985, Fongaro embraced the principles of organic farming and turned into the first organic winery in the Veneto region. To this day Fongaro produces certified organic wines.

Golden yellow, intense and intriguing, with fine bubbles that rise up the glass relentlessly. Notes of bread crust, peanut butter and hazelnut dominate, but as minutes pass, the bouquet is enriched, floral notes accompanied by sensations of exotic fruit and yellow grapefruit and apple come through. Pleasant nuances of vanilla and sweet tobacco further embellish the aromatic framework of the wine.

Dry, taut yet soft and velvety on the palate. The elegant freshness gives way to toasted notes bursting with exotic fruit that give a harmonious and intense flavour. The finish is elegant and well-balanced

Really good with aged cheese, fab with pasta or dumplings or even chicken based dishes. We enjoy it with Indian and Chinese food.
