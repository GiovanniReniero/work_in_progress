Fongaro Spumanti

The Fongaro Winery is nestled in the easternmost section of the Lessini hills, at a stone's throw away from ancienbt roman town of Verona. This remarkable family run estate stands as a staunch advocate for organic farming, and has dedicated its efforts to the production of exceptional Traditional Method Durello sparkling wine.

Established by the visionary Guerrino Fongaro in 1975, the winery placed his bets on the local indigenous grape variety, Durella. This particular vine, of ancient origin, witnessed something of a renaissance in Italy in the late 1960’s when the wines that come from its fruit started to gather noticeable traction nationally and abroad as well.

The unique properties of the Durella grape are pertinent to the rich soil and the cool mountainous environment it thrives in. It is exclusive to the Alpone Valley and grows nowhere else in Italy. The juice from its grapes is vinified through maceration with skins and produces “hard wine for hard people”, as the locals like to boast.

Durella grapes are particularly well suited to the production of exceptional sparkling wines and Fongaro produces some of the better stuff. Fongaro believes in the Traditional Champagne method, all the estates wines are produced this way. Bubbly Bandits finds Fongaro's produce highlight the distinct quality of the Durella grape in a pleasant and exceptionally refined manner.

Fongaro has embraced organic farming for over 4 decades and turned his winery into the first organic winery in the Veneto region, which is of course one of the biggest wine producing regions not only of Italy but the world. To this day Fongaro produces only certified organic wines.

FONGARO-Viola Brut: Golden yellow, intense and intriguing, with fine bubbles that rise relentlessly! Notes of bread, peanut butter and hazelnut are dominate, but if left to rest, the bouquet is enriched by floral notes and nuances of exotic fruit. Grapefruit and apple come through. Pleasant scents of vanilla and sweet tobacco further embellish the aromatic framework of this wine.

Really good with aged cheese. Fabulous with pasta or dumplings or even chicken based dishes. Can be enjoy it with Indian and Chinese food.

FONGARO-Pas Dose: Intense yellow with golden reflections with a lively and persistent perlage.

This pas dosè is elegant and complex with notes of white flowers, oranges, vanilla and raisins. Also, notes of acacia honey.

Goes with everything in BubblyBandit's view, but it's at its best with aged cheeses or a mild Indian curry, pasta (of course) or a not too spicy Mexican taco dish.
