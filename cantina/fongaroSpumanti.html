Fongaro Spumanti

Nestled in the easternmost section of the beautiful, rolling Lessini hills, near a stone's throw away from Verona, lies the boutique Fongaro Winery. This remarkable family run estate stands as a staunch advocate for organic farming, and has dedicated its efforts to the production of exceptional Traditional Method Durello sparkling wine.

Established by the visionary Guerrino Fongaro in 1975, Fongaro Wines established themselves in renouned Roncà village area. This idyllic location rests upon the Tertiary volcanic outcrops of the Lessini region, not far from the renowned townlet of Bolca. Bolca itself has gained fame for its remarkable wealth of well-preserved Eocene fossils, adding an aura of intrigue to the winery's surroundings.

Guerrino placed his bets on the indigenous Lessinia grape variety, Durella. This particular vine, of ancient origin, witnessed something of a renaissance in Italy in the late 1960’s when the wines that come from its fruit started to gather noticeable traction nationally and abroad as well.

The unique properties of the Durella grape are pertinent to the rich soil and the cool mountainous environment it thrives in. The juice from its grapes is vinified through maceration with skins and produces “hard wine for hard people”, as the locals like to boast. Often, it’s watered down or used for blending to increase the acidity of other wines.

Durella grapes are particularly well suited to the production of exceptional sparkling wines and Fongaro produces some of the better stuff! Fongaro believes in the Traditional method, exclusively and Bubbly Bandits finds that Fongaro wines highlight the distinct quality of the Durella grape in a pleasant and very refined manner.

Around 1985, Fongaro embraced organic farming and turned his winery into the first organic winery in the Veneto region, which is of course one of the biggest wine producing regions of Italy and the world. To this day Fongaro produces certified organic wines.

FONGARO-Viola Brut: Golden yellow, intense and intriguing, with fine bubbles that rise up the glass relentlessly! Notes of bread crust, peanut butter and hazelnut dominate, but as minutes pass, the bouquet is enriched, floral notes accompanied by sensations of exotic fruit and yellow grapefruit and apple come through. Pleasant nuances of vanilla and sweet tobacco further embellish the aromatic framework of this wine.

Dry, taut yet soft and velvety on the palate. The elegant freshness gives way to toasted notes bursting with exotic fruit that give a harmonious and intense flavour. The finish is elegant and well-balanced

Really good with aged cheese, fab with pasta or dumplings or even chicken based dishes. We enjoy it with Indian and Chinese food.

FONGARO-Pas Dose: Intense yellow with golden reflections with a ively and persistent perlage.

This pas dosè is elegant and complex with notes of white flowers, oranges, vanilla and raisins. Also, notes of acacia honey.

Goes with everything in BubblyBandit's view, but it's at its best with aged cheeses or a mild Indian curry, pasta (of course) or a not too spicy Mexican taco dish.
