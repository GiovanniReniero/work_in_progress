Peri Bigogno

Graziella and Adriano Peri bought their country house in Castenedolo, Lombardy, in 1946. The historical cellar under the house and the vineyards all around were a strong motivation for Adriano to mantain the tradition of the wine production.

In 2008 their son, Andrea started an important internationalization project that allowed the products after some years, to reach 3 Continents in the world and now are in the UK with the Bandits

In the past centuries Castenedolo has been one of the preferred destinations by the rich and aristocratic families of the city

They used to spend the summer months and enjoy the rural silence of the countryside and vineyards. It is not a coincidence that many of the villas to this day have well-preserved wine cellars decorated in white marble and large oak barrels.

Castenedolo is also near Lake Garda. The Lake plays an essential role as the surrounding territory were generated by four different glaciations, whose result is not just the Lake, but also in the undulated moraine hills and the clay-like plains that surround it. The water of Lago di Garda and the gentle wind that blows in this pre-alpine area, generates the Mediterranean microclimate at the most Northern latitude in the world that has the most amazing effect on the grapes

Peri's Sparkling Chardonnay has a bright straw yellow colour with golden refections. The perlage is fine and persitent almost creamy and so pleasant.

You do get the bread crust aroma moving towards you, though so young in age, but it’s there and surrounded by rich bouquet scents with delecate notes of dry fruit.

Well it’s a Chadonnay so fits well with almost any kind of food.

Peri's Talento Rosè is a fine elegant soft pink rosè, with a fine and persistent perlage.

Notes of nuts, sweet almonds and spices, with hints of wild strawberries blend marvelously together in addition to the aroma of bread crust.

We suggest enjoying it on its own or with appetizers or even pizza.

talento Chardonnay is Yellow with golden reflectons and a very fine and abundant perlage. It’s a pas dosè, so no sugars and not for every palate, but we highly recommend it.

It’s almost a wild sparkly, rich in acidity and aromas and with that lovely perfume of bread crust, and yet it follows its own rules, giving notes ofo hazelnut and dried fruit like peach and apricot and yellow apple.

This Chardonnay goes well with rich dishes and fried food, so just pick your fav and enjoy the pairing.
